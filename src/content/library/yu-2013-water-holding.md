The single most-cited paper in our water-holding claims. Yu et al. ran controlled lab measurements on biochar made from a mix of feedstocks and quantified both the water-holding of pure biochar (over 2.7× dry weight) and the effect of adding biochar to a loamy-sand soil at various inclusion rates.

The key practical finding for Australian operators: in sandy or sandy-loam soils, biochar at a 9% mixture rate doubles the soil's water-holding capacity. That's the number that translates into the 40% irrigation savings reported in turf and parkland trials.
