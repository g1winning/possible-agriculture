The book chapter that mapped out the science of why compost and biochar work better together than either does alone. Cited 380+ times — a foundational reference for anyone designing a composting program.

The mechanism: biochar's porous surface adsorbs ammonia and other volatile nitrogen compounds during active composting, holding them in the windrow instead of letting them escape to the atmosphere. The result is a denser, more nutrient-rich final compost — and biochar that goes into the soil already inoculated with the active microbial community from the compost.

For Australian operators with a serious manure stream — feedlots, dairies, poultry sheds, intensive horticulture — this is the entry point for biochar without changing anything else about your operation. Add 10–20% biochar to the windrow before turning, lose less nitrogen, end up with better compost.
