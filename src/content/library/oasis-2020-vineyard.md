A California Healthy Soils Program field trial on a Pinot Noir vineyard with low-organic-matter sandy soil. Four treatments: control, biochar-only at 10 ton/acre, compost-only at 15 ton/acre, and compost+biochar combined. All received identical irrigation and fertiliser.

First-harvest result for the biochar-only treatment: 45% yield increase over the control, +6 clusters per vine, and Ravaz Index improved into the ideal 5–10 range — indicating both yield and vine balance moved in the right direction simultaneously. At AU equivalent values the economics are similar: a single-season payback on the biochar investment.

The trial is most useful as a transferable case study for any Australian perennial-crop operation on sandy or sandy-loam soils — wine grapes, citrus, orchards, olive groves. The mechanism is water-use efficiency: the trial got the same yield uplift without using any additional irrigation water than the control.
