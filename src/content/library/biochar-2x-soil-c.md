The persistence claim is what differentiates biochar from compost or cover-crop carbon: when compost breaks down the carbon is lost back to the atmosphere within years, but biochar's carbon backbone stays in the soil for decades to centuries. This trial data is one of the field-scale verifications.

Practical implication: a biochar amendment isn't a yearly input you have to keep applying — it's a one-time build of durable soil carbon. That's the property that makes it interesting under ACCU project assessment frameworks.
