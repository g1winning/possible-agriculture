The reason biochar fits so naturally into a composting program: the porous biochar structure adsorbs nutrients, odour-producing compounds, and water from the active compost — so the nitrogen that would otherwise volatilise as ammonia stays in the windrow, the phosphorus that would leach into runoff stays bound to the biochar, and the smell dramatically reduces.

For mixed-livestock operators this is the entry point: feed biochar to the cattle, run the manure through a biochar-amended compost windrow, spread the finished compost back on pasture. The whole nutrient loop closes.
