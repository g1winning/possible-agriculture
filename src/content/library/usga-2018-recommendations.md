The document we point at when superintendents ask about USGA approval. It's a sand-specification document — drainage curves, particle-size distributions, root-zone media targets. Biochar isn't in it.

We've chosen to be straight about this: our biochar is <em>compatible with</em> USGA construction principles, not <em>approved by</em> the USGA. The peer-reviewed water-holding data (Yu et al. 2013) does the real selling on a serious procurement conversation.
