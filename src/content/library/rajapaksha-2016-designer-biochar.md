The authoritative review on biochar modification for environmental remediation. Co-authored by the University of South Australia's Nanthi Bolan, this paper covers every meaningful way to engineer a biochar for a specific contaminant: acid treatment, base treatment, amination, surfactant modification, mineral impregnation, steam activation, magnetic modification.

Practical use case for a council remediation manager: this is the paper you reference when justifying why the biochar going into your contaminated-site treatment isn't generic char from anywhere. The activation profile matters; the matching of modification method to contaminant type matters; the documentation of these mechanisms is what makes biochar a defensible regulatory choice.
