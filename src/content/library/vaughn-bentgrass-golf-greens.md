Direct turf-management research from USDA's Agricultural Research Service. Trials on simulated USGA-spec greens with the standard 30 cm sand root zone over 10 cm pea gravel. Different biochar-compost mixtures evaluated for their effect on creeping bentgrass — the standard cool-season green species in the northern US and high-end Australian courses.

The result that matters for a course superintendent: biochar improved growth without breaking the drainage characteristics that make sand-profile greens function. The Tuckett-style "biochar might destroy your drainage" concern doesn't materialise at the inclusion rates tested here.

For Australian golf courses considering a pilot trial on a back-of-range green or practice fairway, this is the strongest peer-style reference for the principle that biochar can be added to a USGA-spec rootzone without compromising the playing surface.
