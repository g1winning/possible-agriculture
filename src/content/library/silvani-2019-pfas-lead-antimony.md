The companion paper to Sørmo 2021. Same Norwegian team, same activated-biochar approach, but here tested across three contaminant classes simultaneously: PFAS, lead and antimony. Conducted in shooting-range soils with varying organic-matter contents (5.2% and 10.2%).

The headline finding for council and remediation operators: contaminated sites usually have more than one problem. This paper shows that a properly matched biochar — activated coconut-shell for PFAS, iron-enriched for lead — can handle multiple contaminants in one treatment, at inclusion rates that are economically feasible.

The 99% lead reduction is the strongest single remediation result in the activated-biochar literature. For councils dealing with old shooting ranges, fire-training grounds, or other historic-use contamination, this paper is the case for biochar over excavation-and-disposal.
