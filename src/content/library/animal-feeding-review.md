The veterinary-perspective companion to the Leng & Preston cattle work. Where Leng & Preston focused on the production-economics results (live-weight gain, methane), this paper covers the safety, the mechanisms, and the regulatory context.

The mycotoxin-binding aspect is worth attention — it's one of the under-appreciated reasons biochar works in feed: it sequesters toxins that would otherwise harm the gut microbiome, which is part of why feed-conversion improves.
