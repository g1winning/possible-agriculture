The peer-reviewed source for the flame-curtain biochar production method we run on client farms. Cornelissen et al. measured char yield, carbon content, and gas emissions from Kon-Tiki kilns across multiple feedstocks and compared the results against industrial pyrolysis reactors and traditional earth kilns.

The headline findings: char quality fully complies with the European Biochar Certificate (the strictest published standard); CO and NOx emissions are significantly lower than other small-scale production methods; and the capital cost is one-thousandth that of an industrial pyrolysis kiln. The paper is honest about the tradeoff with high-tech industrial reactors, which have "negligible emissions" but are unreachable for the vast majority of farming operations.

For Australian operators with woody-waste streams from storm debris, shelterbelt management or orchard prunings, this is the documented case for converting that material on-site rather than chipping, burning or hauling it.
